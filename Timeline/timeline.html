Typography Timeline

A journey through the types of fonts in different times

  • 1830s

    Removed the serifs of fonts to create a clean, fresh new look. People did not react kindly to removal of serifs, with early sans-serif fonts being known as grotesques.

    "sans" is french for "without"

  • 10 September 1803

    John Walter II, who had succeeded his father as the editor of The Times instituted the use of a new modern typeface that did not include the Long s or any other multi-letter ligatures.

    Jane Austin probably wrote with this extinct letter!

  • 1800s

    Started using fonts to create ads, posters, and large newspaper headlines. Slab fonts are designed to catch your eye, most typewriters use this type of font(pun intended).

  • 1784

    The first Modern font was developed in 1784 by Giambattista Bodoni but didn't become popular until the 20th century. It takes Old Style and transitional to the ultimate conclusion of super fat think strokes and hairline thin strokes with abrupt right angle serifs.

  • 18th Century

    Came around in the Enlightenment period, it takes Old Style a step further by eliminating all traces of Handwritten Calligraphy.

  • Late 15th Century

    First style to emerge in the era of movable type letterpress printing. More sophisticated molding allowed for better contrast between thick and thin strokes and more delicate serif tails.

  • 1460

    Humanist typeface emerges. Based on the writing style of humanist thinkers, it is much lighter and less blocky than Blackletter.

  • 1150 AD - 17th Century

    Blackletter is the oldest typeface. It is a direct descendant of the Carolingian Minuscule, but when books became more popular, scribes needed a more efficient and denser typeface.
